Hossein Narouei-Khandan

Researcher | Scientist

Professional Background

My academic journey began with a bachelor's degree in Plant Pathology from the Agriculture College of Zabol, followed by an M.Sc. focused on using GIS to map plant diseases in Iran. I later earned my Ph.D. in Plant Pathology Modeling from Lincoln University in New Zealand where I worked under supervision of Susan Worner, a great scientist and mentor. Afterward, I completed a postdoc at the University of Florida, collaborating with Professor Ariena van Bruggen and Karen Gareth distinguished scientists in plant pathology, on various research projects.

Research (Click to see more)

My research integrates quantitative modeling, GIS, and bioinformatics to address critical questions at the intersection of plant pathology and environmental science. I focus on the spatial distribution of plant diseases, biosecurity, and the impacts of climate change on agricultural systems. My work involves using advanced statistical techniques and machine learning to predict disease outbreaks, assess risks, and develop strategies for sustainable crop protection.

Affiliation

I am currently affiliated with the Ministry for Primary Industries in New Zealand, where I serve as a Specialist Adviser. In this role, I lead projects focused on biosecurity, spatial intelligence, and risk assessment, utilizing my expertise in GIS, data modeling, and plant pathology to support New Zealand's agricultural and environmental health.

Coding Adventures & Music

In my free time, I enjoy exploring creative outlets through coding and music. I develop small applications and tools for personal projects, experimenting with languages like Python and JavaScript. Additionally, I play the Santur, a Persian traditional intrument ( hammer dulcimer).
